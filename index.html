Research Brief

Healthcare AI: Evaluation & Architecture

Analyzing the gap between generic benchmarks and clinical reality, the risks of open-weight fine-tuning, and the implications of Mixture of Experts (MoE) architectures.

1 Healthcare-Specific Evaluation

Current "passing" scores on medical exams do not correlate with clinical readiness. Generic alignment benchmarks fail to capture the multi-dimensional safety requisite for patient care.

The Evolution of Benchmarks

  • MedMCQA (CHIL 2022) ↗ ~194k questions from Indian medical entrance exams. While it tests domain knowledge, it functions primarily as a trivia retrieval task rather than a clinical workflow simulation.
  • MedQA (USMLE) ↗ Focuses on licensing questions. Frontier LLMs now encounter a "saturation ceiling" here, rendering the metric less useful for distinguishing model capability in real-world scenarios.

Evaluation Maturity

Holistic Frameworks

Moving beyond accuracy to reliability, fairness, and calibration.

  • MedHELM (2025)

    Taxonomy of 5 categories, 22 subcategories, 121 task types. Validated by 29 clinicians.

  • HELM (Bommasani et al.)

    Established multi-axis evaluation as a general principle.

Critical Review

Tam et al. (Nature Digital Medicine) reviewed 142 studies.

Conclusion: Most studies suffer from small samples, weak reliability, and poor generalisability. Current human evaluation is not yet "regulatory grade."

3 Architectural Safety Placement

A fundamental divergence exists in where safety mechanisms are located. GPT/Claude architectures bake safety into the model weights via RLHF, while Mistral's open-weight MoE architecture treats safety as an external, modular component.

Model-Internal vs. Model-External Safety

GPT / Claude Family

Dense Transformer + RLHF
Safety Inside Weights

System cards (from GPT-4o and o1 cards) outline: - Safety training implemented at model training time and system/infrastructure time - Safety mechanisms are NOT a single structural component inside the neural network itself. GPT-4o System Card ↗

Mistral Family

MoE + Open Weights
Safety Outside Model

Safety is an external wrapper, distinct from the raw model weights. Mistral Guardrailing Docs ↗

Mechanistic Reason: Dense

Because every layer touches every token in a dense transformer (GPT-4/Claude), alignment fine-tuning affects all internal pathways. Refusal behaviors become internal "attractor states" within the probability space. Safety is reinforced by, but not solely dependent on, API filters.

Ref: Constitutional AI (Anthropic) ↗

Mechanistic Reason: MoE

In Mixture-of-Experts (Mistral), tokens only activate specific pathways ($k$ out of $N$ experts). Since base weights are released without RLHF, there is no consistent internal anchor for safety. It must be implemented as modular, replaceable filters (gateways or system prompts) by the deployer.

Ref: Mixtral 8x7B Paper ↗

Safety & Governance

Risk Factor

Independent labs report Mistral Pixtral models were significantly more likely than GPT-4o/Claude to output harmful CBRN content under standardized tests.

Mistral provides system-prompt guardrails and moderation endpoints, but these are optional patterns. In local deployment, these can be completely bypassed, placing the governance burden entirely on the institution.
